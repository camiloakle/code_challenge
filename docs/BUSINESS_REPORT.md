### Q5e) Installments Decision Model (Expected Value)

### Correct expected-value formulas implemented

For transactions **with installments**:

- `expected_revenue = (1 - 0.229) * amount + 0.229 * 0.5 * amount`
- `expected_profit = expected_revenue * 0.25`

For transactions **without installments**:

- `expected_profit = amount * 0.25`

### Overall comparison

| Group | Tx Count | Total Amount | Avg Ticket | Expected Revenue | Expected Profit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| with_installments | 3,423,240 | 68.81B | 20,100.18 | 60.93B | 15.23B |
| without_installments | 3,851,127 | 77.42B | 20,103.29 | 77.42B | 19.36B |

Key findings:

- Installments do **not** increase ticket size materially in this dataset (`-0.015%` vs no-installments).
- Expected profit margin with installments is `22.1375%` vs `25.0%` without installments.
- Profit margin penalty is `-2.8625 p.p.` under current risk assumptions.

### Segmented comparison

- By category: each category is almost fully associated with one financing mode in this data (no clean within-category A/B test).
- By city: both financing modes appear in all cities, enabling city-level operational policy tests.
- By category (financing mode mapping): `A` is primarily `without_installments`, while `B`, `C`, and `Unknown category` are primarily `with_installments`.
- By city (pilot targeting): expected-profit delta (`with_installments` minus `without_installments`) varies widely; for controlled installments pilots, prioritize cities with the least negative or even positive delta (e.g., `City_1`, `City_25`, `City_231`, `City_212`, `City_334`) while keeping strict expected-profit guardrails.

Business implication: default risk currently outweighs economics of installments at portfolio level unless installments drive incremental conversion or higher ticket in controlled contexts.
